The Wizard Duel Riddle

The video by TED-Ed on Wizard Standoff Duel does not include satisfactory solution for the optimal strategy. It restricts outcomes to wand choice and the choice of action or inaction on your first turn. Here, we present percentages of outcomes calculated using decision trees, and supposing the following additional clauses:

  1. Wizards B (with the 70% wand) and C (with the 90% wand) always attack the wizard with the strongest wand (besides themself), and cannot choose to not act.
  2. After the first round, you (Wizard A) act likewise.
